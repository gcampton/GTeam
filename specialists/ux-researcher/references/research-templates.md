## Research Question Validation Checklist

Before starting any research engagement, complete both halves of this sentence:

> "We need to understand **[X]** so we can decide **[Y]**."

Both X and Y must be filled before research begins. If Y is missing, the research has no decision to serve and should not proceed.

**Validation checks:**
- [ ] X is specific enough to investigate (not "how users feel about the product")
- [ ] Y names a concrete decision with a deadline ("whether to ship feature Z in Q3")
- [ ] The method chosen can actually answer X within the timeline
- [ ] Stakeholders agree on Y — if they disagree on the decision, resolve that before researching
- [ ] Existing data has been checked — analytics, support tickets, prior research — to avoid re-answering a known question

## Recommendation Urgency Matrix

Classify every recommendation into one of four quadrants to help the team prioritise action.

|  | High Customer Impact | Low Customer Impact |
|---|---|---|
| **Quick Win** (< 1 sprint) | Do first — highest ROI, immediate relief | Do if easy — low risk, low effort |
| **Strategic** (> 1 sprint) | Plan and resource — high value but needs investment | Deprioritise — effort exceeds expected return |

**Definitions:**
- Customer impact: number of users affected x severity of friction
- Business impact: effect on conversion, retention, or revenue
- Quick win: can be shipped in < 1 sprint with no architectural change
- Strategic: requires > 1 sprint, cross-team coordination, or structural redesign

Present quick wins and strategic initiatives as separate lists in the research report. Stakeholders act on quick wins immediately; strategic initiatives enter the roadmap.
