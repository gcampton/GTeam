## AGITATION

Here's what's actually happening when your systems are scattered:

**You're leaking revenue.** That client who asked for "one more round of revisions" three rounds ago? Without a scope tracker, you absorbed the cost. Multiply that across a few projects and you've given away thousands in free work this year.

**You're losing clients to slow follow-up.** The proposal you meant to send on Monday went out on Thursday. By then, they'd already hired someone else. Not because they were better — because they were faster.

**You're undercharging because you can't see your own numbers.** When you don't track time accurately or know your real project profitability, you price based on gut feel. Gut feel almost always undercharges.

**You're working more hours, not earning more money.** Admin work expands to fill the gaps between paid work. The busier you get, the more time you spend managing the business instead of doing the business.

One freelancer we spoke to tracked her time for a week. She spent 11 hours on client work and 14 hours on admin, email, and "trying to find things." She was running a business where the overhead had quietly overtaken the output.

That's not a freelance career. That's a job with worse benefits.
