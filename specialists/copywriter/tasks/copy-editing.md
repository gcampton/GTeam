## Copy Editing & Rewrites

**Use when:** Reviewing existing copy for clarity, conversion, and voice alignment — not writing from scratch.

**Gather:** The draft, what it's for (sales page / email / ad), audience, voice guide (if any), what specifically feels off (or full diagnostic if unspecified).

**Diagnostic pass — flag each of these:**

- **Vague benefits** — any claim that could apply to a competitor. Replace with specifics ("saves time" → "cuts onboarding from 2 weeks to 3 days").
- **Marketing-speak** — "leverage", "synergy", "cutting-edge", "best-in-class", "world-class". Strike or rewrite.
- **Feature-dumping** — bullet lists of features with no benefit translation. Apply FAB to each.
- **Weak headlines** — fails the "so what?" test. Does not name a transformation, audience, or specific outcome.
- **Buried CTAs** — the action is unclear, passive, or hidden. Make it action-verb + immediate benefit.
- **Over-hedging** — "might", "could", "can help you to potentially". Commit to the claim or cut it.
- **Long sentences** — anything > 25 words in body copy. Break it.
- **Passive voice** — "mistakes were made" → "we made mistakes". Re-voice unless the passive is intentional.

**Deliver:**
- Annotated draft: each issue flagged with line-level comment
- Rewritten version (full clean copy)
- Summary of the top 3 fixes and why each lifts conversion or clarity
