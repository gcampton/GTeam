# Niche Scoring Rubric

Score each candidate niche across 4 dimensions (1–5 each). Max score: 20. Target ≥ 14 before committing.

## Dimension 1: Demand Score (1–5)

Does enough people want this? Is it growing?

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|---------|
| 5 | High search volume (10k+/month on primary keyword) + trending upward on Google Trends |
| 4 | Moderate volume (2k–10k/month) + stable or growing trend |
| 3 | Moderate volume but flat trend, or high volume but declining |
| 2 | Low volume (< 2k/month) but clear upward trend (early opportunity) |
| 1 | Low volume + flat/declining trend |

**Check:** Google Trends direction, search volume estimate via WebSearch, Reddit activity (active subreddits = demand signal)

## Dimension 2: Competition Score (1–5)

How hard is it to get visible in this niche?

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|---------|
| 5 | Top results are thin affiliate sites or low-quality blogs (DA < 30); allintitle < 5,000 |
| 4 | Mix of thin sites and one or two authority sites; beatable with good content |
| 3 | Some strong competitors (DA 40–60) but clear content gaps exist |
| 2 | Dominated by DA 60+ brands but a specific sub-niche is open |
| 1 | Dominated by Amazon, Wikipedia, major media — no realistic organic entry point |

**Check:** `allintitle:"<keyword>"` count, top 5 result domain quality, YouTube channel sub counts for video niches

## Dimension 3: Monetisation Score (1–5)

How much can you make per visitor / subscriber / viewer?

| Score | Model | Criteria |
|-------|-------|---------|
| 5 | Affiliate | Commission ≥ £50/sale OR ≥ 30% recurring SaaS commission |
| 5 | YouTube | CPM ≥ £8 (finance, software, B2B, legal, insurance) |
| 5 | Digital product | Clear willingness to pay ≥ £20/month subscription |
| 4 | Affiliate | Commission £20–50/sale |
| 4 | YouTube | CPM £4–8 (health, fitness, how-to, education) |
| 3 | Dropship | Gross margin 35–50% with clear product-market fit |
| 3 | Affiliate | Commission £10–20/sale but high conversion rate |
| 2 | YouTube | CPM £2–4 (general lifestyle, cooking, travel) |
| 1 | Any | Commission < £5, CPM < £2, or no clear monetisation path found |

## Dimension 4: Effort Score (1–5)

How much time/money to first income?

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|---------|
| 5 | First income possible within 30 days; minimal upfront cost (< £50); existing skills applicable |
| 4 | First income in 60–90 days; low cost; one new skill needed |
| 3 | First income in 3–6 months; moderate cost (£100–500); some learning curve |
| 2 | First income in 6–12 months; higher cost or significant skill gap |
| 1 | First income 12+ months; requires significant capital or team |

## Composite Score Interpretation

| Total | Recommendation |
|-------|---------------|
| 18–20 | **Strong go** — high confidence, start immediately |
| 14–17 | **Go** — solid opportunity, proceed with first action |
| 10–13 | **Conditional** — identify which dimension is weak; fix it or find a sub-niche |
| < 10 | **Pass** — too much friction; move to next candidate |

## Red Flags (automatic disqualifiers regardless of score)

- Niche requires medical/legal/financial advice + no credentials = YMYL risk
- Supplier ships direct from China only with 3–6 week delivery times (kills conversion)
- YouTube niche where top 10 channels all have > 500k subs and upload daily
- Affiliate program has closed or has < 6 months history (collapse risk)
- Google Trends showing sharp drop in the last 3 months (timing is wrong)
