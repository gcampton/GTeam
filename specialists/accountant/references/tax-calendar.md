# Tax Calendar & Obligations

> **Confidence:** All dates are `[HYPOTHESIS]` based on standard rules — verify current year dates with HMRC, IRS, or relevant authority before advising a client. Dates shift when they fall on weekends/public holidays. Late filing and payment penalties are listed where known.

---

## United Kingdom

### Corporation Tax

`[HYPOTHESIS]`

| Obligation | When Due | Notes |
|-----------|---------|-------|
| CT600 filing | 12 months after accounting period end | HMRC online via Government Gateway |
| Corporation tax payment (small companies) | 9 months and 1 day after accounting period end | "Small company" = taxable profits < £1.5M |
| CT payment — quarterly instalments (large companies) | Months 7, 10, 13, 16 of accounting period | Applies if taxable profits > £1.5M (adjusted threshold for groups) |
| Amended CT600 | Within 12 months of original filing deadline | |

**Common penalty triggers:**
- Late filing: £100 (immediate), £200 (3 months late), tax-geared penalty after 6 months (10%) and 12 months (further 10–20%)
- Late payment: interest charged from due date; surcharge for persistent late payers
- `[HYPOTHESIS]` Flag: Companies that change year-end often miscalculate the 9-month payment deadline — verify the accounting period end date, not the company year end

### VAT

`[HYPOTHESIS]`

| Obligation | When Due | Notes |
|-----------|---------|-------|
| Quarterly VAT return (standard) | 1 month + 7 days after quarter end | e.g. Q ending 31 March → due 7 May |
| Monthly VAT return | 1 month + 7 days after month end | Available on request; repayment traders prefer this |
| Annual Accounting Scheme | 2 months after year end; 9 monthly interim payments | Available for turnover < £1.35M |
| Making Tax Digital (MTD) for VAT | All VAT-registered businesses must file via MTD-compatible software | Mandatory since April 2022 |
| VAT registration threshold | Within 30 days of exceeding £90,000 rolling 12-month turnover (2024/25 rate) | Check current threshold — changes annually |

**Common penalty triggers:**
- Late filing or payment (new points-based system from January 2023): accumulate points → financial penalty
- VAT surcharge (old system): applies to businesses on surcharge regime from before Jan 2023
- `[HYPOTHESIS]` Flag: MTD non-compliance (filing via non-approved software) — HMRC taking increasing enforcement action

### PAYE & National Insurance

`[HYPOTHESIS]`

| Obligation | When Due | Notes |
|-----------|---------|-------|
| Monthly PAYE/NIC payment | 19th of following month (postal); 22nd (electronic) | Applies to most employers |
| Quarterly PAYE (small employers) | 19th/22nd after end of quarter | Only if average monthly PAYE liability < £1,500 |
| Full Payment Submission (FPS) | On or before each payday | Real Time Information (RTI) submission to HMRC |
| Employer Payment Summary (EPS) | By 19th of following month | Required when no payments made, or to claim reductions |
| P60 to employees | By 31 May following tax year end (5 April) | Physical or electronic |
| P11D (benefits in kind) | 6 July following tax year end | File P11D for each employee with benefits; pay Class 1A NIC by 19 July (22 July electronic) |
| P11D(b) — Class 1A NIC | 6 July (filing); 19/22 July (payment) | Employer return for all Class 1A NIC |

**Common penalty triggers:**
- Late FPS: penalties from £100–£400 per month depending on number of employees (after first late submission per year)
- Late P11D: £300 per P11D + daily penalty of £60 if continued
- `[HYPOTHESIS]` Flag proactively: P11D is commonly missed — benefits in kind include company cars, private medical insurance, interest-free loans over £10,000, gym memberships

### Self Assessment (Individuals / Sole Traders / Partners)

`[HYPOTHESIS]`

| Obligation | When Due | Notes |
|-----------|---------|-------|
| Online SA return | 31 January following tax year end (year ends 5 April) | e.g. 2023/24 return → due 31 January 2025 |
| Paper SA return | 31 October following tax year end | |
| Payment on account — 1st instalment | 31 January | Based on prior year tax liability |
| Payment on account — 2nd instalment | 31 July | Based on prior year tax liability |
| Balancing payment | 31 January | Final settlement of prior year |
| CGT on property disposals | 60 days after completion | Separate CGT return required |

**Common penalty triggers:**
- 1 day late: £100 automatic penalty
- 3 months late: £10/day (up to 90 days = £900)
- 6 months late: 5% of tax due or £300 (whichever greater)
- 12 months late: further 5% or £300 + potential 30/70/100% tax-geared penalty for deliberate withholding
- Late payment interest: Bank of England base rate + 2.5% (check current rate)
- `[HYPOTHESIS]` Payments on account are frequently underestimated — if income rises significantly, client may face large balancing payment and penalties

## United States

### Federal Corporate Tax

`[HYPOTHESIS]`

| Obligation | When Due | Notes |
|-----------|---------|-------|
| Form 1120 (C-Corp) | 15th day of 4th month after tax year end | e.g. December year end → 15 April; can extend to 15 October |
| Form 1120-S (S-Corp) | 15th day of 3rd month after tax year end | e.g. December year end → 15 March; can extend to 15 September |
| Form 1065 (Partnership) | 15th day of 3rd month after tax year end | Pass-through; partners report on personal returns |
| Extension request | Filed by original due date | Extends filing, NOT payment — estimated tax still due by original date |

**Estimated Quarterly Tax Payments (Corporations):**

`[HYPOTHESIS]`

| Quarter | Payment Due |
|---------|------------|
| Q1 (Jan–Mar) | 15 April |
| Q2 (Apr–Jun) | 15 June |
| Q3 (Jul–Sep) | 15 September |
| Q4 (Oct–Dec) | 15 December |

- Required if estimated tax liability ≥ $500 (corporations) or $1,000 (individuals)
- Safe harbour: pay 100% of prior year tax (110% if prior year AGI > $150,000 for individuals) to avoid underpayment penalty

### Information Returns

`[HYPOTHESIS]`

| Form | Due Date | What It Covers |
|------|---------|----------------|
| W-2 (employee wages) | 31 January (to employee + SSA) | All employee compensation |
| 1099-NEC (non-employee compensation) | 31 January (to recipient + IRS) | Payments ≥ $600 to contractors |
| 1099-MISC | 31 January (if Box 8/10); otherwise 28 February paper / 31 March electronic | Rents, royalties, other income |
| 1099-K (payment card transactions) | 31 January | Third-party payment networks (threshold: $2,500 in 2025, $600 from 2026 — check IRS) |
| 1042-S (foreign persons' income) | 15 March | Withholding on US-sourced income paid to foreign persons |
| FBAR (FinCEN 114) | 15 April (automatic extension to 15 October) | Foreign bank accounts with aggregate > $10,000 |

**Common penalty triggers:**
- Failure to file W-2/1099 on time: $60–$310 per form (inflation-adjusted; check current rates)
- Failure to furnish payee copy: additional $310 per form
- Intentional disregard: $630+ per form — no cap
- `[HYPOTHESIS]` Flag proactively: Companies using Stripe/PayPal for payments to contractors — 1099-K thresholds are changing; platform may file separately but company should reconcile

### State and Local Taxes

`[HYPOTHESIS]` Flag these situationally:

- State income tax: varies — California (up to 8.84% corporate), Texas (no income tax, but franchise tax), New York (6.5% + NYC surcharge)
- Sales tax nexus: physical presence OR economic nexus (typically > $100,000 sales or > 200 transactions in a state)
- `[HYPOTHESIS]` Flag: any company selling into multiple US states without a nexus study — Wayfair decision (2018) means economic nexus is broadly enforceable; exposure can be significant

## EU VAT — Cross-Border Rules

`[HYPOTHESIS]`

### One Stop Shop (OSS) Scheme

| Scheme | Who Uses It | What It Covers |
|--------|------------|----------------|
| Union OSS | EU-established businesses | B2C digital services + goods sold to EU consumers |
| Non-Union OSS | Non-EU businesses | B2C digital services to EU consumers |
| Import OSS (IOSS) | Any seller | Goods in consignments ≤ €150 imported and sold to EU consumers |

- **Filing:** Quarterly OSS return; payment due end of month following quarter end
- Replaces need to register for VAT in each EU member state (for qualifying supplies)
- `[HYPOTHESIS]` Flag: UK businesses post-Brexit are Non-Union scheme eligible for EU B2C digital services — register in any single EU member state

### Distance Selling

- EU-wide threshold from July 2021: €10,000 pan-EU. Below threshold: charge VAT at seller's country rate. Above: charge VAT at buyer's country rate (use OSS to file)
- `[HYPOTHESIS]` Flag: Any UK/US e-commerce business selling goods to EU consumers — check whether OSS registration is needed; non-compliance creates VAT liability in every EU member state where sales occurred

### B2B Reverse Charge

- B2B supplies to VAT-registered EU customers: supplier does not charge VAT; customer self-accounts (reverse charge)
- Supplier must: obtain and verify customer's VAT number; include "reverse charge applies" on invoice; report in EC Sales List (some member states still require this post-2022 — verify)

## Common Penalty Triggers — Quick Proactive Flag List

`[HYPOTHESIS]` Flag these before the client hits them:

| Trigger | Jurisdiction | What Happens | When to Flag |
|---------|-------------|-------------|-------------|
| Missed CT payment deadline | UK | Interest from due date; surcharge risk | 60 days before year end +9 months |
| Missed P11D for company cars/health insurance | UK | £300/form + daily penalties | Annually at tax year end |
| MTD VAT non-compliance | UK | Points + financial penalties | Before first MTD filing period |
| PAYE underpayment (IR35 misclassification) | UK | Back taxes + interest + penalties + NIC | On any contractor review |
| Missing 1099-NEC for contractor payments | US | $60–$310/form | January each year |
| Economic nexus in additional US states | US | Multi-state sales tax exposure | When sales into new states begin |
| FBAR non-filing | US | $10,000+/year civil penalty per account | When foreign bank accounts confirmed |
| OSS/IOSS non-registration | EU | VAT liability in each member state | When EU consumer sales begin |
| EU distance selling threshold exceeded | EU | VAT due in destination country | Monitor rolling 12-month EU sales |
| Payments on account underestimated (SA) | UK | Interest + late payment surcharge | When client's income rises significantly |
| P60 not issued by 31 May | UK | PAYE compliance failure | Payroll calendar reminder |
| 1099-K threshold changes missed | US | Underreporting of contractor payments | Annually in Q4 planning |
