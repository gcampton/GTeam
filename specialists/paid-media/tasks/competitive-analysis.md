## Competitive Ad Analysis

**Use when:** Planning a new campaign, entering a new market, or refreshing creative. Research what competitors are running before designing your own ads.

**Ad library sources:**

| Platform | Where to Look | What You Get |
|---|---|---|
| Meta (Facebook/Instagram) | [Facebook Ad Library](https://www.facebook.com/ads/library/) | All active ads, launch date, platforms, page info |
| LinkedIn | Company page → "Ads" tab | Sponsored content visible to all |
| Google Ads | [Google Ads Transparency Center](https://adstransparency.google.com/) | Search, display, and YouTube ads by advertiser |
| TikTok | [TikTok Creative Center](https://ads.tiktok.com/business/creativecenter/) | Top-performing ads by industry, trending formats |

**Analysis workflow:**

1. **Extract** — pull all active ads from 3-5 direct competitors. Screenshot or save each ad. Organise by competitor → platform → date.

2. **Categorise** — group ads by:
   - **Theme:** product feature, pain point, social proof, promotional, educational
   - **Format:** static image, video, carousel, UGC-style, testimonial
   - **Audience:** who the ad seems to target (segment by messaging tone and offer)
   - **Funnel stage:** awareness (broad benefit) vs consideration (comparison/proof) vs conversion (CTA/offer)

3. **Identify patterns** — across the competitor set:
   - What pain points appear in 3+ competitors' ads? (market-validated messaging)
   - What formats dominate? (the market has tested this for you)
   - What's the typical headline structure? (benefit-first, question, statistic, contrarian?)
   - What CTAs are used? ("Try free", "Book demo", "Learn more", "Shop now")
   - What proof elements appear? (logos, star ratings, user counts, testimonials)

4. **Find gaps** — what are competitors NOT saying?
   - Pain points your product addresses that competitors ignore
   - Audience segments competitors aren't targeting
   - Formats competitors aren't using (e.g., everyone does static, nobody does UGC)
   - Objections nobody is addressing in their ads

5. **Generate insights** — for each finding, explain WHY it likely works:
   - "3 of 5 competitors lead with time-saving → this pain point has broad resonance"
   - "Nobody addresses data security → opportunity to differentiate in enterprise segment"

**Output — Competitive Ad Report:**

```markdown
# Competitive Ad Analysis: [Market/Category]

## Competitors Analysed
| Company | Platform(s) | Active Ads | Primary Theme |
|---|---|---|---|
| [Name] | Meta, LinkedIn | 23 | [Theme] |

## Common Patterns
- [Pattern 1 with frequency across competitors]
- [Pattern 2]

## Messaging Themes (ranked by frequency)
1. [Pain point / value prop] — used by X/Y competitors
2. [Pain point / value prop]

## Creative Patterns
- Dominant format: [format] ([X]% of ads)
- Top-performing structure: [description]

## Gaps & Opportunities
1. [Gap] → [How to exploit it]
2. [Gap] → [How to exploit it]

## Recommended Test Ads (3-5)
Based on proven patterns + identified gaps:
1. [Ad concept with hook, key message, format, CTA]
2. [Ad concept]
```

**Cadence:** Run competitive analysis quarterly, or before any major campaign launch. Save reports to `results/` for longitudinal comparison — track how competitor messaging evolves over time.
