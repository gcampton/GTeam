# Competitive Ad Analysis Patterns

## Ad Copy Formulas That Win (Cross-Industry)

Based on analysis of high-performing ads across SaaS, e-commerce, and B2B [HYPOTHESIS]:

### Headline Structures

| Structure | Example | Best For |
|---|---|---|
| Problem → Solution | "Tired of spreadsheet chaos? → One dashboard for everything" | Pain-point-aware audience |
| Specific Number | "Cut meeting time by 50%" | Credibility, bookmarks |
| Social Proof | "Join 20,000 teams who..." | Conversion campaigns |
| Contrarian | "You don't need a CRM" | Attention, engagement |
| How-to | "How to [outcome] in [timeframe]" | Educational, traffic |
| Question | "Still tracking projects in email?" | Problem awareness |

### Body Copy Patterns

| Pattern | When It Works |
|---|---|
| Problem → Agitation → Solution | Cold audiences who don't know you |
| Feature → Benefit → Proof | Consideration-stage audiences comparing options |
| Testimonial → CTA | Retargeting audiences who need final push |
| Before/After | Visual products, transformation offers |
| List of 3 | Feature highlights ("Fast. Simple. Secure.") |

### CTA Patterns by Funnel Stage

| Stage | CTA | Why |
|---|---|---|
| Awareness | "Learn more", "See how" | Low commitment, builds interest |
| Consideration | "Try free", "Book a demo", "Compare plans" | Invites evaluation |
| Conversion | "Start free trial", "Get started", "Buy now" | Direct action |
| Retention | "Upgrade", "Unlock [feature]", "Renew" | Value-focused |

## Visual Patterns

### What Works by Platform [HYPOTHESIS]

| Platform | Top-Performing Format | Why |
|---|---|---|
| Meta Feed | UGC-style video (selfie cam, casual) | Feels native, bypasses ad blindness |
| Meta Stories/Reels | 9:16 video with text overlay, first 2s hook | Full-screen, sound-off default |
| LinkedIn | Single image with bold text overlay | Professional feed, skimmable |
| Google Display | Clean product shot on solid background | Stands out in cluttered placements |
| TikTok | Creator-led, trending audio, raw feel | Platform rewards native content |
| YouTube Pre-roll | Hook in 5 seconds (before skip button) | Captive attention window |

### Before/After Ad Pattern

Most effective for:
- SaaS (messy workflow → clean dashboard)
- E-commerce (problem state → product in use)
- Services (struggling → thriving)

Structure: Left side = pain (red, cluttered, stressed). Right side = solution (green, clean, happy). Single line of copy bridging them.

## Competitor Analysis Signals

### What Ad Frequency Tells You

| Signal | What It Means |
|---|---|
| Same ad running 6+ months | This ad is profitable — study it carefully |
| Many variants of same message | They're testing aggressively — the MESSAGE is validated, format is being optimised |
| New ads every 2-3 weeks | Active testing programme — look at what they keep vs what they kill |
| One ad only, rarely changed | Low investment in paid — opportunity to outspend |
| Different messages per platform | Sophisticated operation — study platform-specific adaptation |

### What Ad Count Tells You

| Count | Interpretation |
|---|---|
| 1-5 active ads | Small operation or early testing |
| 10-20 active ads | Established programme, likely profitable |
| 50+ active ads | Mature, scaled operation — study their structure |
| 100+ active ads | Enterprise-level with dedicated creative team |

## Ethical Guidelines

- Use competitive analysis for **inspiration and market understanding**, never plagiarism
- Adapt patterns and principles, don't copy headlines or visuals
- Competitors' ads show you what the market responds to — your job is to do it better and more authentically
- Save and date your analyses — tracking how competitor messaging evolves reveals market shifts before they're obvious
