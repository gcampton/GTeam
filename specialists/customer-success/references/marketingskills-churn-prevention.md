# Churn Prevention Guide

This comprehensive resource covers strategies for reducing both voluntary churn (customer-initiated cancellations) and involuntary churn (failed payments).

## Core Framework

The guide structures churn prevention around two parallel tracks:

**Voluntary Churn Prevention:**
The recommended cancel flow follows this sequence: customer triggers cancellation → exit survey → dynamic save offer → confirmation → post-cancel engagement. The exit survey asks "What's the main reason you're cancelling?" to identify which targeted intervention works best.

**Involuntary Churn Recovery:**
The payment recovery approach uses smart retries combined with escalating dunning emails. The system retries soft declines multiple times over 7-10 days while immediately requesting card updates for hard declines.

## Key Strategic Insights

**Match offers to reasons.** A discount doesn't solve "missing feature" problems, and a pause doesn't help someone who can't afford the service. The guide maps specific cancellation reasons to appropriate interventions:
- Price concerns → discounts or downgrades
- Low usage → pause or onboarding help
- Missing features → roadmap preview
- Technical issues → immediate support escalation

**Involuntary churn is easier to fix.** While voluntary churn represents 50-70% of total churn, involuntary churn (30-50%) has recovery rates of 50-60% with proper dunning sequences.

**Track leading indicators before cancellation.** Login frequency drops, feature usage changes, and support ticket patterns can identify at-risk customers weeks before they cancel, enabling proactive interventions.

## Measurement Targets

Healthy metrics include cancel flow save rates of 25-35%, dunning recovery rates of 50-60%, and pause reactivation rates of 60-80%.
