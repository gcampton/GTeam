# Social Media Platform Specifications

> **Confidence:** All recommendations are `[HYPOTHESIS]` (untested best-practice) unless marked `[TESTED: date]` or `[REVISED: date]`. Check `../results/` for empirical outcomes before applying advice.

Platform specs change without notice. Treat sizes as current best practice and verify against official documentation before a major campaign. Character limits and algorithm behaviour are the most volatile — image dimensions are more stable.

## LinkedIn

### Image Specs

| Format | Dimensions | Notes |
|--------|-----------|-------|
| Link preview / shared image | 1200 × 627px | 1.91:1 ratio |
| Carousel (document post) | 1080 × 1080px (recommended) | Supports any aspect ratio — square performs best in feed |
| Profile photo | 400 × 400px minimum | Displays at 200×200px |
| Cover / banner | 1584 × 396px | |
| Company page logo | 300 × 300px | |

### Video Specs

- Max file size: 256MB
- Duration: 3 seconds – 10 minutes (in-feed); 3 seconds – 60 minutes (Live)
- Recommended format: MP4, H.264
- Aspect ratio: square (1:1) recommended for feed; 16:9 acceptable; 9:16 (vertical) available but less common in professional context
- Captions: upload SRT file — auto-captions available but imperfect

### Text Limits

- Post character limit: 3,000 characters
- Visible before "see more": approximately 150–220 characters (mobile); the hook must land here
- Article headline: 150 characters
- Comment: 1,250 characters

### Algorithm Notes

`[HYPOTHESIS]` LinkedIn's engagement hierarchy (approximate, inferred from public creator reports):

**Rewarded:** Comments > reactions > shares > saves > dwell time (time spent reading/watching)

**Deprioritised:**
- Outbound links in the post body — post text with link in first comment is the common workaround; effectiveness is debated
- Low dwell time (posts scrolled past quickly signal low quality to the algorithm)
- Reposting without adding original commentary

**Rewarded content formats (in rough order as of 2024–2025):**
1. Native documents (carousels)
2. Video (especially with captions)
3. Text-only posts with strong hook
4. Single image with context in caption

### Best Times `[HYPOTHESIS]`

| Day | Times (audience local time) |
|-----|---------------------------|
| Tuesday – Thursday | 8–10am and 12pm |
| Monday | 8am (acceptable, less competitive) |
| Avoid | Weekends, Friday afternoon |

## Instagram

### Image Specs

| Format | Dimensions | Aspect ratio |
|--------|-----------|-------------|
| Feed square | 1080 × 1080px | 1:1 |
| Feed portrait (recommended for reach) | 1080 × 1350px | 4:5 |
| Feed landscape | 1080 × 566px | 1.91:1 |
| Stories | 1080 × 1920px | 9:16 |
| Reels | 1080 × 1920px | 9:16 |
| Reels cover thumbnail | 1080 × 1920px (displayed at 420×654px) | 9:16 |

### Video Specs

| Format | Duration | Notes |
|--------|---------|-------|
| Reels | 15 seconds – 90 seconds ideal; up to 15 minutes | Hook in first 3 frames |
| Stories video | 15 seconds per card | Auto-splits longer videos |
| Feed video | Up to 60 minutes | Reels format preferred for discovery |

### Text Limits

- Caption: 2,200 characters
- Visible before "more": approximately 125 characters (mobile) — hook must land here
- Bio: 150 characters
- Username: 30 characters

### Hashtag Strategy

`[HYPOTHESIS]` The 30-hashtag era is over. Instagram's algorithm has deprioritised hashtag volume in favour of content quality and watch time.

**Current recommended approach:** 3–10 hashtags, mix of:
- 1–2 broad (100k+ posts): reach
- 3–5 niche (1k–50k posts): relevant audience, less competition
- 1 branded: community building

Avoid hashtag stuffing (20–30 tags). It signals low-quality posting and may not provide incremental reach.

### Algorithm Notes

`[HYPOTHESIS]`

**Rewarded:** Shares (DM shares are the strongest signal), saves, comments, watch time (Reels)

**Deprioritised:**
- Outbound links (no links in captions — use bio link or link sticker in Stories)
- Low watch time on Reels (viewers dropping in first 3 seconds is a negative signal)
- Re-shared TikTok content with watermarks (explicitly stated in Meta's guidelines)

**Reels distribution logic:** Content is first shown to a sample of followers; engagement rate in that sample determines broader distribution. A Reel that underperforms with your followers will not be distributed to non-followers.

## X (Twitter)

### Character Limits

- Standard tweet: 280 characters (free accounts); 25,000 characters (X Premium / verified)
- For most marketing use: write for 280-character constraint regardless of account tier — shorter posts perform better
- Bio: 160 characters
- Display name: 50 characters

### Image Specs

| Format | Dimensions / Ratio | Notes |
|--------|-------------------|-------|
| In-line image | 16:9 to 4:1 acceptable; 1200 × 675px recommended | Displayed cropped in timeline — place key content in centre |
| Profile photo | 400 × 400px | Circular crop |
| Header / banner | 1500 × 500px | |

### Video Specs

- Max duration: 140 seconds (2 minutes 20 seconds)
- Max file size: 512MB
- Recommended format: MP4, H.264
- Aspect ratio: 16:9, 1:1, or 9:16

### Polls

- Up to 4 answer options
- Duration: 5 minutes to 7 days (5 days standard)
- Polls cannot be edited after posting

### Algorithm Notes

`[HYPOTHESIS]`

**Rewarded:** Replies and quote tweets generate more algorithmic reach than retweets. Engagement that keeps users on the platform (replies, quote engagement threads) is rewarded over passive engagement (likes).

**Deprioritised:**
- External links are explicitly suppressed in distribution — link-in-replies or link-in-comments workaround (post the link as a reply to your own tweet) is widely used
- Coordinated inauthentic engagement (follow/unfollow loops, engagement pods)

**Content that performs:** Bold contrarian takes, data reveals, real-time commentary on trending topics, numbered lists in threads.

## TikTok

### Video Specs

- Aspect ratio: 9:16 (vertical, full-screen) — mandatory for full distribution
- Recommended resolution: 1080 × 1920px
- Duration: 15 seconds – 10 minutes; **60 seconds – 3 minutes is the sweet spot** for watch completion
- Max file size: 287.6MB
- Format: MP4 or MOV

### Caption and Text Limits

- Caption: 2,200 characters
- Effective use: 1–2 sentences in caption + relevant hashtags; detailed copy belongs in the video itself
- Bio: 80 characters

### Algorithm Notes

`[HYPOTHESIS]` TikTok's algorithm is the most aggressive content-quality filter of any major platform — it distributes to non-followers by default and uses completion rate and re-watch rate as primary quality signals.

**The first 3 seconds are everything:** The hook must be delivered before the viewer can scroll. This means:
- The verbal hook must be spoken, not building up to
- On-screen text for the hook should appear within the first frame
- Visual hook: something that creates a "wait, what?" reaction before the 3-second mark

**Rewarded:** Watch completion, re-watches, shares (especially off-platform), comments (especially replies to creator's comments)

**Deprioritised:**
- TikTok watermarked videos posted to Instagram/YouTube Shorts (cross-platform suppression is algorithmic)
- Low completion rate (if viewers consistently drop at the same point, the algorithm pulls distribution)

## Facebook

### Image Specs

| Format | Dimensions | Notes |
|--------|-----------|-------|
| Feed image | 1200 × 630px | |
| Stories | 1080 × 1920px | 9:16 |
| Event cover | 1920 × 1080px | |
| Group cover | 1640 × 856px | |

### Video Specs

- Recommended: MP4, H.264
- Aspect ratio: 16:9 (landscape) or 9:16 (vertical); 1:1 works well in feed
- Max file size: 10GB; max duration: 240 minutes
- Reels (Facebook): same 9:16 spec as Instagram Reels

### Content Performance Notes

`[HYPOTHESIS]`

**Mixed content formats perform:** Unlike Instagram (visual-first) or LinkedIn (professional/text-first), Facebook feed rewards variety — image posts, video, link posts, and text-only posts all have audiences on Facebook.

**Groups outperform Pages:** Organic reach for Facebook Pages has declined significantly. Groups maintain higher organic reach because they deliver notifications and appear in a dedicated tab. For community-based brands, a Group is more effective than a Page for engagement.

**Events:** For local, in-person, or webinar-style events, Facebook Events still drives meaningful attendance discovery — especially for an older demographic (35+). Use Events alongside other announcement channels, not instead of them.

**Outbound links:** Facebook does not algorithmically suppress outbound links as aggressively as some platforms, but native video and images outperform link-preview posts in feed distribution.

## Platform Comparison Quick-Reference

| Platform | Primary format | Outbound links in post | Algorithm priority signal |
|----------|--------------|----------------------|--------------------------|
| LinkedIn | Text + document carousel | Suppressed in body (use comments) | Comments, dwell time |
| Instagram | Visual + Reels | Not supported (bio/Stories only) | Shares, saves, watch time |
| X (Twitter) | Text + threads | Suppressed in tweet body | Replies, quote tweets |
| TikTok | Vertical video | Not applicable (link in bio only) | Completion rate, re-watches |
| Facebook | Mixed (video preferred) | Supported but native beats links | Video watch time, Group engagement |
