# Social Media Content Strategy

> **Confidence:** All recommendations are `[HYPOTHESIS]` (untested best-practice) unless marked `[TESTED: date]` or `[REVISED: date]`. Check `../results/` for empirical outcomes before applying advice.

Strategy before tactics. Define your content ratio, your series formats, and your repurposing chain before deciding what to post today.

---

## The 80/20 Content Ratio

80% of content should be educational or entertaining. 20% should be promotional.

**What this looks like in a monthly content calendar (20 posts/month):**

| Type | Count | Examples |
|------|-------|---------|
| Educational | 10 | How-to, frameworks, data insights, explainers |
| Entertaining / engaging | 6 | Behind-the-scenes, opinion, community questions, polls |
| Promotional | 4 | Product launch, offer, event, demo request |

**The reasoning:** Audiences follow accounts that teach them or entertain them. An account that posts promotions more than 20% of the time will be unfollowed or muted. Promotional posts get more traction when they arrive in a feed the audience already trusts.

`[HYPOTHESIS]` The 80/20 split is a starting point, not a law. B2B SaaS audiences on LinkedIn tolerate a higher promotional ratio (closer to 70/30) because the content is inherently professional and buyers expect product signals. B2C audiences on Instagram are less tolerant — the split may need to be 90/10. Adjust based on engagement data, not doctrine.

**Practical calendar rule:** For every promotional post, plan 4 educational or entertaining posts around it. Schedule the promotional post after the surrounding content has warmed the audience.

## Content Series

Recurring themes build audiences faster than one-off posts. Pattern recognition creates anticipation — followers learn to look forward to a specific format.

**Why series work:**

1. **Consistency signals reliability.** An audience that sees "#HotTake Friday" every week learns to check in.
2. **Templates reduce production friction.** A defined format is faster to produce than a blank-canvas post.
3. **Algorithmic benefit.** Consistent posting behaviour and engagement patterns on a series reinforce account quality signals.

### 5 Series Formats

| Format | Description | Cadence | Platforms |
|--------|------------|---------|-----------|
| **How-to** | Step-by-step on a specific skill or task. "How to do X in 5 steps." | Weekly | LinkedIn, Instagram, TikTok |
| **Behind-the-scenes** | Show the process, the team, the work-in-progress. Not just the polished outcome. | Weekly or biweekly | Instagram, TikTok, LinkedIn |
| **Hot takes** | Contrarian or non-obvious opinions on industry topics. Designed to provoke genuine discussion. | Weekly | LinkedIn, X (Twitter) |
| **Q&A** | Answer audience or community questions. DM-sourced questions perform better than invented ones. | Weekly or biweekly | Instagram Stories, LinkedIn, TikTok |
| **Case study** | Break down a real result: what the problem was, what was tried, what worked. Numbers required. | Monthly | LinkedIn, Instagram, newsletter |

`[HYPOTHESIS]` Case studies drive the highest-quality engagement (comments from buyers and practitioners) but require the most production effort. Hot takes drive the highest volume of engagement but attract more noise. Build a mix: one case study series, one hot-take series, one lighter-weight format.

## Hook Types by Platform

The hook is the first thing a viewer sees, reads, or hears. It must earn the next moment of attention. Hook strategy varies by platform because reading/viewing behaviours differ.

### LinkedIn

**Format:** Text-first feed; reader decides whether to click "see more" after the first 2–3 lines.

**Hook pattern:** Bold first line that makes a claim or poses a question the reader wants resolved.

```
Good: "Most LinkedIn advice is wrong."
Good: "We grew from 0 to 10k followers in 6 months. Here's exactly what we did."
Good: "The question I ask every founder in our first call."
Weak: "Excited to share our latest thinking on content marketing..."
Weak: "I've been reflecting a lot lately about..."
```

`[HYPOTHESIS]` The bold first line followed by a line break (then a second hook sentence before "see more") performs better than a single long opening. The line break creates visual air and implies there's more to read.

### X (Twitter)

**Format:** Short, public, skimmable. Retweets and quote-tweets are the primary distribution mechanism.

**Hook pattern:** Contrarian take or surprising claim that's short enough to read in a glance and strong enough to provoke a response.

```
Good: "Most 'growth hacks' are just things that worked once, for someone else, in 2019."
Good: "Your onboarding email isn't your onboarding. Your first 10 minutes of product use is."
Weak: "Here are some thoughts on content strategy [thread 🧵]"
```

`[HYPOTHESIS]` On X, threads perform better when the first tweet works as a standalone statement — the reader clicks to see the thread because the opener was valuable, not because they were instructed to.

### Instagram

**Format:** Visual-first feed; the image or video thumbnail is the hook before any text is read.

**Hook pattern:** The visual communicates the value before the caption is read. For Reels, the on-screen text in the first 3 frames is the hook.

```
Image hook: Bold text overlay with a claim ("The 5-slide deck that closed a $500k deal")
Reels hook frame 1: Text on screen says "Things your accountant won't tell you" before anything is spoken
Caption hook: First line assumes they've already been intrigued by the visual
Weak: A stock photo with no text, relying entirely on the caption for context
```

### TikTok

**Format:** Video-first, vertical, full-screen, autoplay. The viewer is mid-scroll and will not wait.

**Hook pattern:** The spoken hook must be delivered in the first 3 seconds — before the viewer's thumb moves. On-screen text and verbal hook must align and appear simultaneously.

```
Good: Video starts mid-action. Creator is already talking: "Nobody tells you this about pricing your services..."
Good: Text on screen at frame 1: "POV: you asked me how I made $80k freelancing this year"
Weak: 2 seconds of intro music before anything substantive appears
Weak: "Hey guys, today I'm going to talk about..."
```

`[HYPOTHESIS]` "Pattern interrupt" hooks — unusual visuals, unexpected sounds, or text that breaks the expected format — perform above average on TikTok because the algorithm rewards completion rate, and disrupting the scroll pattern increases completion.

## Engagement Baiting vs. Genuine Engagement

**What crosses the line (platform-policy violation and trust damage):**
- "Like this post if you agree, comment if you disagree" — explicit engagement bait, suppressed by LinkedIn and Meta
- "Comment 'YES' to get the template" — algorithmic manipulation, increasingly suppressed
- "Tag 3 friends to enter" — allowed on Meta with restrictions, but damages audience trust and list quality
- Comment pods (coordinated inauthentic engagement between accounts) — violates ToS on all major platforms

`[HYPOTHESIS]` Engagement bait produces a short-term engagement spike that trains the algorithm to expect that engagement level. When normal posts don't maintain that level, distribution may actually decrease relative to pre-bait baseline.

**What's acceptable and effective:**
- Asking a genuine question at the end of a post that relates to the content ("What's the biggest blocker you see here?")
- Polls with real answer options (not "Option A: Yes / Option B: Also Yes")
- "Save this for later" (only if the content genuinely warrants saving — checklists, frameworks, templates)
- Asking people to share if they found it useful (once, not in every post)

## Hashtag Strategy

Use hashtags to reach adjacent audiences, not to game the algorithm. On most platforms in 2024–2025, hashtag reach has declined — content quality distributes posts, not hashtag volume.

**The three-tier approach:**

| Tier | Post volume | Purpose | Count |
|------|------------|---------|-------|
| Broad | 100k+ posts | Maximum reach; high competition | 1–2 |
| Niche | 1k–50k posts | Relevant audience, lower competition | 3–5 |
| Branded | Any | Community building, campaign tracking | 1 |

**Example (content marketing niche):**
```
Broad:    #ContentMarketing (900k+ posts)
          #MarketingTips (500k+ posts)
Niche:    #B2BContentStrategy (8k posts)
          #SaaSMarketing (20k posts)
          #ContentOps (3k posts)
Branded:  #[YourBrand]Tips
```

`[HYPOTHESIS]` Niche hashtags deliver more qualified followers per impression than broad hashtags. A post appearing in a 5,000-post hashtag is more likely to be seen by someone genuinely interested in that specific topic than a post buried in a 900,000-post hashtag.

**Platform-specific hashtag notes:**

| Platform | Recommended count | Notes |
|----------|------------------|-------|
| LinkedIn | 3–5 | Hashtags are clickable but not a primary discovery mechanism |
| Instagram | 3–10 | Post-era of 30 hashtags; quality over quantity |
| X (Twitter) | 1–2 max | More than 2 reads as spam; often none in thread-style posts |
| TikTok | 3–5 | Mix niche + broad; include #fyp sparingly (debated effectiveness) |
| Facebook | 0–3 | Hashtags have limited function on Facebook |

## Content Repurposing Chain

Create once, distribute everywhere. The chain starts with the highest-effort format and waterfalls into shorter formats.

```
[LONG-FORM BLOG POST or VIDEO INTERVIEW]
        ↓
[LinkedIn carousel] — 8–12 slides, key frameworks from the article
        ↓
[Twitter/X thread] — key insights as standalone points, 5–10 tweets
        ↓
[TikTok / Instagram Reels] — 60–90s video, one insight from the article
        ↓
[Newsletter section] — summary + link to original, with personal commentary
        ↓
[Instagram quote cards or infographic] — single data point or quote, shareable visual
```

`[HYPOTHESIS]` The repurposing chain works in one direction: start with the richest format, compress for shorter platforms. Going from TikTok to long-form blog is harder and results in thin content. Invest in one long-form asset per week and redistribute; don't create six separate short-form pieces from scratch.

**Repurposing checklist per long-form asset:**
- [ ] Extract 3–5 standalone insights suitable for social posts
- [ ] Identify the one most shareable data point or quote
- [ ] Create carousel/thread version within 24 hours of publication (while the content is fresh for the writer)
- [ ] Schedule short-form video for 3–7 days after blog publish (captures search-driven readers and social-first audience separately)

## Posting Cadence

### Growth mode (building audience from scratch)

| Platform | Cadence |
|----------|---------|
| LinkedIn | 4–5x per week |
| Instagram | 1 Reel + 3–4 Stories per week |
| X (Twitter) | 1–3 tweets per day; 1 thread per week |
| TikTok | 1–2 videos per day |
| Facebook | 3–4x per week |

### Maintenance mode (established audience, moderate resources)

| Platform | Cadence |
|----------|---------|
| LinkedIn | 2–3x per week |
| Instagram | 3–4x per week (mix Reels and carousels) |
| X (Twitter) | 3–5 tweets per week |
| TikTok | 3–5 videos per week |
| Facebook | 2–3x per week |

### Minimal presence (keeping accounts active, low resource allocation)

| Platform | Cadence |
|----------|---------|
| LinkedIn | 1x per week |
| Instagram | 2x per week |
| X (Twitter) | 2–3x per week |
| TikTok | 2x per week |
| Facebook | 1–2x per week |

`[HYPOTHESIS]` Consistent posting at a sustainable cadence outperforms burst-posting followed by silence. An account that posts reliably 3x/week builds faster than one that posts 15x one week and disappears for three weeks. Choose a cadence you can maintain for 90 days without burning out — that's the right cadence, regardless of what the "optimal" frequency advice says.
