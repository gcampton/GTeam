# Pricing Page Conversion Reference

## Hierarchy of Impact

Fix these elements in order — early items have 5–10× the conversion impact of later ones.

1. Number of tiers (3 is optimal)
2. Clear recommended/highlighted tier
3. Headline and subheadline clarity
4. CTA copy and placement
5. Annual billing toggle visibility
6. Social proof per tier
7. Feature list readability
8. FAQ section
9. Trust signals (security, payment logos, guarantee)
10. Mobile experience

---

## Page Structure

### Above the Fold (most critical)
- **Headline:** What problem does this solve? Not your product name — the outcome. E.g. "Simple pricing that grows with your team" or "Start free, pay as you scale."
- **Subheadline:** One-sentence clarification of the offer. "No credit card required" or "14-day free trial, cancel anytime."
- **Annual/monthly toggle:** Above the tier table, not below it. Show the % savings prominently: "Save 20% with annual."
- **Default selection:** Default to annual pricing. Monthly should be the opt-in, not the default.

### Tier Table
- **Order:** Display tiers left to right from lowest to highest price (or cheapest to most expensive). This is the standard — don't reverse it.
- **Highlighting:** The recommended tier should be visually distinct: badge ("Most Popular" / "Best Value"), subtle background colour, slightly larger card, or outlined border.
- **Price display:** Show the per-month price for both annual and monthly. For annual, show "billed annually" in smaller text. Do NOT show just the annual total — "$948/year" is harder to evaluate than "$79/month billed annually."
- **Feature list:** 5–8 key features per tier. Use checkmarks for included, grey-out or omit for excluded. Never use ✗ symbols — they create negative associations.
- **Tier diff highlighting:** Use a different colour or bolding for features that are NEW in each tier (helps customers see what they're upgrading to).
- **CTA button per tier:** One button per tier. Copy matters:
  - Entry/free: "Start for free" (not "Sign up")
  - Recommended: "Start free trial" or "Get started"
  - Business: "Start free trial" or "Try Business"
  - Enterprise: "Contact sales" or "Talk to us"

### Below the Tier Table
- **Money-back guarantee / trial terms:** "14-day free trial, no credit card required" OR "30-day money-back guarantee" stated clearly
- **Logos:** 6–8 recognisable customer logos (if you have them). "Trusted by teams at [Logos]" — builds confidence at the decision moment
- **1–2 testimonials:** Ideally one from each target segment (e.g. a startup and a mid-market company). Attribute with name, title, company, photo if available.
- **FAQ section:** Minimum 6 questions; see FAQ template below
- **Enterprise callout:** A distinct row or card at the bottom for Enterprise if you have custom pricing. "Need more? [Contact sales →]"

## Annual vs. Monthly Toggle

### Discount sizing
- **15% off annual:** Too small to move the needle. Most customers don't feel the difference.
- **20% off annual:** Standard SaaS. Clearly meaningful, round number that's easy to communicate.
- **25–30% off annual:** Strong incentive, common for products that compete on price. Risk: trains customers to wait for bigger discounts.
- **>30% off annual:** Usually signals pricing problems elsewhere (monthly price too high, or annual not valued). Avoid unless strategic.

### Toggle design
- Placed above the tier cards, centre-aligned
- "Monthly | Annual" with the default pre-selected highlighted
- Annual option shows savings: "Annual (Save 20%)" not just "Annual"
- When toggled, prices update dynamically without page reload

### What to show when Annual is selected
Show the per-month equivalent, not the annual total:
- ✓ "$79/month, billed annually"
- ✗ "$948/year"

The annual total can be shown as secondary text: "(That's $948/year)" — helps enterprise buyers who budget annually.

## FAQ Template (Pricing Page)

These 8 questions cover 90% of pricing page objections. Use verbatim customer language wherever possible.

**1. Is there a free trial?**
"Yes — every plan comes with a [14]-day free trial. No credit card required. If you upgrade to a paid plan and change your mind within [30] days, we'll refund you in full."

**2. Can I change plans later?**
"Yes. You can upgrade or downgrade at any time from your account settings. Upgrades take effect immediately. Downgrades take effect at the next billing cycle."

**3. What payment methods do you accept?**
"We accept all major credit cards (Visa, Mastercard, Amex) and [PayPal/ACH/invoicing for annual plans >$X]."

**4. Do you offer discounts for nonprofits or startups?**
"Yes — [specific discount details] for verified nonprofits and early-stage startups. [Email/apply link]."

**5. What happens to my data if I cancel?**
"Your data is yours. You can export everything before cancelling. We retain your data for [30 days] after cancellation in case you change your mind, then permanently delete it."

**6. Is there a contract?**
"Monthly plans have no contract — cancel anytime. Annual plans are paid upfront for 12 months and are non-refundable after [30 days]. No auto-renewal surprises — we'll email you [30 days] before renewal."

**7. Do you offer volume or team discounts?**
"Yes — pricing automatically scales as you add seats. For large teams (50+ users) or custom contracts, [contact our sales team]."

**8. What kind of support is included?**
"[Free/Starter]: [Support level]. [Recommended]: [Support level]. [Business]: [Support level with SLA]. Enterprise customers get a dedicated CSM."

## Social Proof Placement

### At the tier level
Testimonials placed directly below or beside the tier they came from convert better than a generic "what customers say" section. If you have a testimonial from an SMB team, place it near the Starter tier. Enterprise testimonials near the Business/Enterprise tier.

### Logos
Place before the testimonials. Logos provide instant credibility signal; testimonials deliver the proof. Pattern: "Trusted by teams at [Logos]" → [2 testimonials] → [FAQ]

### Review count
If you have G2 or Capterra ratings: "★★★★★ 4.7/5 from 340 reviews on G2" performs well near the CTA and in the FAQ section.

## Mobile Pricing Page Checklist

- [ ] Tier cards stack vertically (not side by side — too small to read)
- [ ] Recommended tier appears first (or most prominently) in the stack
- [ ] CTA buttons are thumb-sized (≥ 44px height)
- [ ] Annual toggle is visible before scrolling
- [ ] Feature checkmarks are legible at 14px minimum
- [ ] FAQ section collapses (accordion) — full FAQ text on mobile is too long
- [ ] Page load < 3s on 4G (pricing page load speed directly impacts conversion)
