# SaaS Pricing Benchmarks

## Price Point Benchmarks by Segment

### SMB SaaS ($0–$50K ACV)
| Tier | Typical range | Notes |
|------|--------------|-------|
| Free / Freemium | $0 | 2–5% convert to paid (industry average) |
| Entry paid | $9–$29/mo per seat | Solo users or small teams |
| Recommended | $49–$99/mo per seat | Full team features |
| Business | $149–$299/mo per seat | Admin, SSO, compliance |

### Mid-market SaaS ($50K–$250K ACV)
| Tier | Typical range | Notes |
|------|--------------|-------|
| Entry | $25–$79/mo per seat | Rarely free — qualify customers |
| Recommended | $99–$199/mo per seat | Core value proposition |
| Business | $300–$600/mo per seat | Enterprise-lite features |
| Enterprise | Custom | $50K–$250K ACV, negotiated |

### Enterprise SaaS (>$250K ACV)
- Primarily custom pricing and negotiated contracts
- Platform fee ($25K–$150K/year) + per seat
- Minimum commitment common (100 seats minimum, etc.)
- Multi-year contracts with 10–15% annual uplift clauses standard

## Annual vs. Monthly Mix Benchmarks

| Company stage | % on annual plans | Target |
|--------------|-------------------|--------|
| Early-stage (<$1M ARR) | 20–35% | Push annual with strong discounts |
| Growth-stage ($1M–$10M ARR) | 35–55% | Optimise annual toggle conversion |
| Scale-stage (>$10M ARR) | 50–70% | Annual is the default expectation |

Annual customers have significantly lower churn:
- Monthly plan churn: 3–7%/month (typical SMB SaaS)
- Annual plan churn: 15–25%/year (at renewal point only)
- Implication: 1 annual customer is worth 3–4× a monthly customer in LTV, even after the 20% annual discount

## Revenue Metrics Benchmarks (B2B SaaS)

| Metric | Early-stage | Growth | Scale |
|--------|-------------|--------|-------|
| ARPU (per customer) | $50–$200/mo | $200–$1,000/mo | $1,000+/mo |
| NRR (Net Revenue Retention) | 90–100% | 105–115% | 115–130% |
| GRR (Gross Revenue Retention) | 80–90% | 85–92% | 88–95% |
| LTV:CAC ratio | >2:1 | >3:1 | >4:1 |
| CAC payback period | <24mo | <18mo | <12mo |
| Expansion revenue % of new ARR | 5–15% | 20–40% | 40–60% |

## Pricing Page Conversion Benchmarks

| Traffic source | Typical pricing page → trial/purchase conversion |
|---------------|--------------------------------------------------|
| Organic search | 3–6% |
| Paid search | 6–12% |
| Email (nurture) | 8–15% |
| Direct / brand | 5–10% |
| Product-led (in-app upgrade) | 15–25% |

**Annual toggle adoption:** 25–45% of visitors who start on monthly toggle to annual when the discount is displayed clearly. If below 20%, the discount isn't compelling enough or the toggle isn't prominent.

## Freemium Benchmarks

| Metric | Benchmark range |
|--------|----------------|
| Free-to-paid conversion rate | 2–8% |
| Median time to convert | 30–90 days |
| % of free users who ever upgrade | 5–15% (most never will) |
| Revenue from freemium-originated users at 12mo | 30–50% of new ARR for PLG products |

**Freemium viability test:** If your CAC for self-serve conversions is >$200 and your free plan has meaningful support costs, freemium may be cash-flow negative until ARR > $5M. Run the unit economics before committing.

## Price Increase Benchmarks

| Increase size | Typical churn impact | Recovery timeline |
|--------------|---------------------|-------------------|
| 5–10% | 0–2% incremental churn | Immediate net positive |
| 15–20% | 3–7% incremental churn | Positive within 90 days |
| 25–35% | 8–15% incremental churn | Positive within 6 months |
| >40% | 15–30% incremental churn | High risk — requires pilot first |

**Rule of thumb:** If NPS > 40 and "price" is not in your top 3 churn reasons, a 15–20% price increase will be cash-flow positive within 2–3 months even with churn.

## Competitor Pricing: How to Find It

1. **Direct visit:** Most SaaS companies publish their pricing pages. Check `/pricing` on the competitor domain.
2. **Wayback Machine:** Historical pricing at `web.archive.org/web/*/competitor.com/pricing`
3. **G2/Capterra:** Review sites often have pricing summaries and user comments about price changes.
4. **LinkedIn Sales Navigator:** Competitor pricing is often discussed in sales community posts.
5. **Industry reports:** Bessemer, OpenView, KeyBanc publish annual SaaS pricing surveys.

When competitors hide pricing ("Contact sales" only), they're likely:
- Dealing with complex configuration (enterprise)
- Testing price sensitivity with each prospect
- Embarrassed about price relative to perceived value

If a major competitor hides pricing, consider whether publishing your pricing creates a trust advantage over them.
