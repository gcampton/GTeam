### Tuesday, Apr 14 — Framework / Playbook (Carousel)

**Hook (Slide 1):** How to Write a Cold Email That Actually Gets Replies (B2B Edition)

**Slides:**
1. **Cover:** How to Write a Cold Email That Actually Gets Replies
2. **The reality:** Average cold email reply rate is 1–3%. Top performers hit 15–25%. The difference is structure.
3. **Line 1 — Relevance signal:** Reference something specific about their company. Not "I love what you're doing." Try: "Saw you just expanded into [market] — that usually creates [problem]."
4. **Line 2 — Problem statement:** Name the pain they're likely feeling. Be specific. "Most [role] at [company size] spend X hours on Y."
5. **Line 3 — Proof you solve it:** One sentence. One result. "[Company] cut that from X to Y in Z weeks."
6. **Line 4 — Low-friction CTA:** Not "book a 30-min demo." Try: "Worth a 10-min look?" or "Want me to send a 2-min video of how it works?"
7. **What to avoid:** Paragraphs. Attachments. "I hope this email finds you well." Anything over 80 words.
8. **CTA:** Screenshot this. Use it for your next outbound campaign.

**Caption:** I've tested 200+ cold email variations. The ones that work all follow this structure. Not because it's magic — because it respects the reader's time. Full framework in the carousel ↓

**Hashtags:** #ColdEmail #B2BSales #Outbound
**Visual brief:** Carousel, clean layout, each slide = one principle, example text highlighted

## Content Series Concepts (Recurring Formats)

### 1. "Behind the Build" (Weekly — Thursdays)
Founder or product lead shares a real decision, mistake, or trade-off from building the product. Raw, specific, no PR polish. These consistently drive the highest engagement on B2B LinkedIn because they're rare.

### 2. "Framework Friday" (Bi-weekly — alternating Fridays)
Actionable carousel breaking down a specific business framework the audience can use immediately. Save rate is the target metric — these compound over time as LinkedIn resurfaces saved content.

### 3. "Metric of the Month" (Monthly — first Monday)
Deep-dive text post on one SaaS metric. What it is, why it matters, how to calculate it, what good looks like, common mistakes. Positions the brand as data-literate and builds a library of reference content.

## Engagement Protocol

**Post-publish (first 60 minutes):**
- Respond to every comment within the first hour — this is when LinkedIn decides whether to boost distribution
- Ask a follow-up question in each reply to keep the thread going
- Share the post to relevant LinkedIn groups (if applicable)

**Daily (15 minutes):**
- Comment meaningfully on 5 posts from target audience or adjacent founders
- Never "Great post!" — add a specific insight, counterpoint, or question

**Weekly:**
- Review which posts crossed 3% engagement rate
- Note which formats and topics over/underperformed
- Adjust next week's topics based on signal
