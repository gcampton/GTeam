### Community Engagement

**Use when:** Building an ongoing engagement strategy for a community, reviving a quiet community, or levelling up an active one. Engagement is not a one-time event — it's a system.

**Gather:** Community platform, member count, current activity level (messages/day), community purpose, existing engagement efforts (if any), team capacity for content creation, member demographics (professional, hobbyist, student, mixed).

---

### Engagement Rhythms

Structure engagement around predictable daily, weekly, and monthly cadences. Members should know what to expect and when.

**Daily:**
- Respond to every new member introduction within 4 hours
- Seed at least one discussion prompt or question if channels are quiet
- React to or reply to member-shared content (don't let posts die with zero engagement)

**Weekly:**
- Monday: "What are you working on this week?" thread
- Wednesday: Resource share, tutorial, or tip of the week
- Friday: Wins thread ("What went well this week?") or casual open chat
- One scheduled event (rotating: AMA, workshop, challenge, co-working)

**Monthly:**
- Member spotlight feature (profile a contributor, interview them)
- Community retrospective: what worked, what didn't, what to try next month
- Content roundup: best posts, resources, and discussions from the past month
- Metrics review (see health-check.md)

### Event Types

Not every event works for every community. Match the format to your audience.

| Event | Best For | Effort | Duration | Platform |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| AMA (Ask Me Anything) | Expert communities, product teams | Medium | 1-2 hours | Discord stage, Reddit, Discourse |
| Workshop / Tutorial | Learning communities, dev communities | High | 1-3 hours | Discord voice + screen share, Zoom |
| Challenge / Hackathon | Creator, dev, design communities | Medium | 1-7 days async | Any (post results in thread) |
| Co-working session | Freelancer, indie, productivity communities | Low | 2-4 hours | Discord voice (cameras optional) |
| Game night / Social | Gaming, general interest communities | Low | 1-2 hours | Discord voice + activity |
| Show and tell | Maker, creator, dev communities | Low | 30-60 min | Text channel or voice |
| Debate / Discussion panel | Professional, academic communities | Medium | 1 hour | Discord stage, Slack huddle |
| Office hours | Product communities, support-heavy | Low | 1 hour weekly | Discord voice, Slack huddle |

**Event execution checklist:**
- [ ] Announce 7 days in advance with date, time (include timezone), and what to expect
- [ ] Create a dedicated channel or thread for the event
- [ ] Remind 24 hours before and 1 hour before
- [ ] Assign a host and a moderator (separate roles)
- [ ] Record or summarise the event for members who couldn't attend
- [ ] Post a follow-up thread for continued discussion
- [ ] Collect feedback: "What did you think? What should we do next?"

### Content Programming

Don't rely on members to create all content. Seed discussions, curate resources, and create recurring formats.

**Discussion prompts that actually work:**
- Specific, not generic: "What's one tool you started using this year that changed your workflow?" beats "What tools do you use?"
- Opinion-inviting: "Hot take: [controversial-but-safe statement]. Agree or disagree?"
- Experience-based: "What's the worst mistake you made early in [field] and what did you learn?"
- Low-barrier: "Share your current desk/workspace setup" (photo prompts get high engagement)

**Discussion prompts that flop:**
- Too broad: "What do you think about AI?"
- Too niche too early: "What's your take on the latest RFC for HTTP/3 QPACK?"
- Requiring effort with no payoff: "Write a 500-word essay on your career journey"
- Already been done to death: "Introduce yourself!" (after the first month, nobody cares)

**Polls:** Use sparingly. One poll per week maximum. Best for: "What topic should our next AMA cover?", "Which day works best for events?", "What content do you want more of?" Not for: engagement farming ("What's your favourite colour?").

**Member spotlights:** Pick one active member per month. Ask them 5 questions about their work, share it as a formatted post. This rewards contribution and gives quieter members a model to follow.

### Member Journey

Design a clear path from new member to community champion. Each stage should feel like a natural progression, not a grind.

| Stage | Behaviour | Recognition | Unlock |
|---|---|---|---|
| **New** | Joins, reads rules, posts intro | Welcome message, first reaction from a mod | Access to all public channels |
| **Active** | Posts regularly, replies to others (50+ messages) | @Active role, colour | Access to voice channels, image posting |
| **Contributor** | Helps others, shares resources, starts discussions (200+ messages) | @Contributor role, mention in spotlight | Access to beta channels, event hosting |
| **Moderator** | Recruited from contributors, trained | @Moderator role, mod tools | Mod channel access, decision-making input |
| **Champion** | Consistently shapes community culture, mentors others | @Champion role, direct line to leadership | Advisory role, early access to decisions |

**Transition triggers:** Don't auto-promote purely on message count. Message count is a prerequisite, but promotion should require a mod review of contribution quality. 500 low-effort messages is not the same as 200 helpful ones.

### Gamification

Gamification works when it reinforces behaviours you already want. It backfires when it becomes the reason people participate.

**What works:**
- **Levels tied to meaningful roles** — Level 5 unlocks voice channels, Level 15 unlocks a custom role colour. The unlock must feel useful, not decorative.
- **Badges for specific achievements** — "First PR merged", "Helped 10 members", "Attended 5 events". Achievement-based, not grind-based.
- **Leaderboards for short bursts** — Weekly leaderboard for a challenge, then reset. Permanent leaderboards create anxiety and discourage new members.
- **XP for quality, not quantity** — If possible, weight XP toward reactions received (others valued your message) rather than raw message count.

**What backfires:**
- **Permanent leaderboards** — The same 5 people dominate, everyone else feels invisible
- **XP for raw message count** — Encourages spam and low-effort posting
- **Too many badges** — If everything is rewarded, nothing feels special
- **Pay-to-win perks** — Paid members getting visible advantages over free members breeds resentment (cosmetic perks are fine)

**Implementation (Discord):**
- Use Arcane or MEE6 for levelling (Arcane preferred — better XP curves, less upselling)
- Create role rewards at meaningful thresholds (not every 5 levels)
- Announce level-ups in a dedicated #level-ups channel, not #general (it becomes noise)

### Cross-Platform Engagement

A community doesn't live on one platform. Use social media to drive awareness and funnel members to the core platform.

**Strategy:**
- Share the best community discussions on Twitter/X (with member permission and credit)
- Post event announcements on LinkedIn, Twitter, and relevant subreddits
- Create short clips from voice events for YouTube Shorts or TikTok
- Use an email newsletter for the monthly roundup — catches members who don't check the platform daily

**Rule:** Social media promotes the community. The community does not promote social media. Don't ask members to follow your Twitter — ask Twitter followers to join your community.

### Feedback Collection

You can't improve what you don't measure, and metrics alone don't tell you why.

**Quarterly survey (5-7 questions, takes < 3 minutes):**
1. How would you rate your experience in this community? (1-5)
2. What's one thing you'd change about this community?
3. What content or events would you like to see more of?
4. Do you feel welcome and included here? (Yes / Mostly / Not really / No)
5. How likely are you to recommend this community to a colleague? (0-10 NPS)
6. Any other feedback?

**Suggestion channel:** Create a #suggestions channel where members can post ideas. React to every suggestion within 48 hours — even if the answer is "not right now." Ignoring suggestions is worse than saying no.

**Retrospectives (for the mod team):**
- Monthly: What went well? What didn't? What should we try?
- After every event: attendance, engagement quality, feedback received, what to improve
- After every incident: what happened, how we responded, what to change

**Deliver:**
- Engagement calendar for the next 30 days (daily/weekly/monthly rhythm filled in)
- 3 events planned with dates, formats, and hosts assigned
- 20 discussion prompts ready to deploy (not generic — tailored to this community)
- Member journey stages defined with roles, thresholds, and perks
- Gamification system configured (if appropriate for the community)
- Feedback survey drafted and ready to deploy
- Cross-platform promotion plan with channels and posting schedule
